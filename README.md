# Teaching Gen AI

Welcome to the **Teaching Gen AI** repository. This material is designed to provide a foundational understanding of Generative AI, its relationship with traditional AI, and why it is a critical skill for the future.

---

## 1. Introduction to Generative AI
Generative AI (GenAI) is a modern form of AI that differs from traditional AI by its ability to **generate new content** based on user queries. Unlike systems with pre-decided answers, GenAI creates unique content every time, including:

* **Text** (Articles, code, stories)
* **Images** (Digital art, diagrams)
* **Videos** (Animations, realistic clips)
* **Audio** (Music, synthesized speech)
* **Programming Code** (Python, Java, etc.)

---

## 2. Understanding the AI Hierarchy
To understand GenAI, it is essential to distinguish it from its predecessors: Machine Learning (ML) and Deep Learning (DL).



| Category | Definition | Real-World Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Machine Learning (ML)** | Systems that learn from previous experiences and behaviors to predict future outcomes. | **Netflix Recommendations:** Tracks your watch history to predict what you might like next. |
| **Deep Learning (DL)** | A subset of ML where the machine learns complex patterns or deep architectures. | **Face Unlock:** Analyzes complex patterns like eye shape and facial geometry to recognize you. |
| **Generative AI (GenAI)** | Systems that do not just predict or recognize, but create entirely new data. | **Chatbots (e.g., Gemini):** Generates a unique response rather than pulling from a fixed database. |

> **Key Distinction:** Traditional AI (ML and DL) focuses on learning from the past to **predict or classify**, while Modern AI (GenAI) focuses on **creation**.

---

## 3. Core Generative Models
GenAI is powered by various types of models depending on the output required:

* **LLM (Large Language Model):** Machines trained on massive text data. *Examples: Gemini, ChatGPT, Claude.*
* **LIM (Large Image Model):** Systems that generate images. *Example: Google Imagen / Nano.*
* **LVM (Large Vision Model):** Systems capable of generating images and videos. *Example: Google Veo.*
* **LAM (Large Audio Model):** Models designed specifically to generate or manipulate audio.

## 4. Why Learn GenAI in 2026?
The industry has shifted. While GenAI was once an optional "good-to-have" skill, it is now a **mandatory skill** for the IT industry.

### Key Industry Trends:
1.  **Universal Adoption:** Every major company is currently adopting AI into their workflow.
2.  **Job Requirements:** AI skills are becoming a staple in standard job descriptions.
3.  **Efficiency:** Developers are integrating GenAI to significantly improve coding productivity.
